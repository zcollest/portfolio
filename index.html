About Me

My Background

Hello! My name is Zach Collester, and I am a second-year MSc candidate in Bioinformatics for Health Sciences at Universitat Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona, Spain. I'm originally from the Philadelphia area (Go Sixers!) of the United States and graduated from Bates College (Lewiston, ME, US) in 2019 with a BS in Neuroscience. I am curious about the intersection of biomedicine and industry, specifically with respect to genetic diseases. I am also exploring the concept of "civic hacking" by utilizing open source data to generate insights for the public good. Outside of the computational world, I am generally consumed by music, basketball, and desparately trying to improve my Spanish.

1. Masters Thesis

I am currently conducting an industrial thesis with Sequentia Biotech, a bioinformatics startup in Barcelona. My internship contract precludes me from displaying my code or discussing my project in substantial detail; however, my thesis generally involves utilizing various machine learning models (random forest, gradient boost, neural nets, etc.) to model both cancer and normal transcriptome RNASeq data. Data science packages in both Python (Pandas, Scikit-learn, Keras, Tensorflow) as well as R (Tidyverse) are used.

2. Macro-Complex Modeling of Biomolecules

Worked in a group of three to develop a python-based application that utilizes pairwise interaction data as input to generate macro-complex models of biomolecules. It functions for both protein-protein interactions (PPIs) as well as protein-DNA & protein- RNA interactions. The output is a PDB file of the generated model. The package is available here.

3. Exploring MA Public School Data

Using some of the skills that I learned from my Master's program, I conducted a small project using Massachusetts public school district data. One of my closest friends serves as a guidance counselor at a Boston public school, and the main goal was to uncover variables related to college attendance rates, specifically with respect to race and funding. This project involved basic data mining / wrangling, exploratory data analysis, & multivariate regression modeling, and I created a simple website to display the project.

4. Differential Gene Expression Analysis

I carried out a differential gene expression analysis as part of a masters course called "Information Extraction in Omics Technology." The Rmarkdown report uses RNASeq count data from Henry et al. (2018) in an attempt to better understand pathways related to noncanonical NF-kB signaling. The report includes quality assessment / preprocessing, differential gene expression analysis, as well as a functional enrichment analysis, and utilizes edgeR, sva, and SummarizedExperiment packages.

5. Bachelors Thesis in Neurobiology

I have linked to a PDF of my year-long laboratory research thesis within the Bates College Neuroscience Department. Investigation of the IRBIT protein was carried out via drug-induced knock-downs and calcium imaging. The goal of the project was to elucidate the IRBIT protein's role in vitro, ultimately expanding our knowledge of dynamic calcium signaling processes in the brain.
