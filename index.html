About Me

My Background

Hello! Welcome to my portfolio website. My name is Zach Collester, and I am a second-year MSc candidate in Bioinformatics for Health Sciences at Universitat Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona, Spain. I am initially from the Philadelphia area of the United States and graduated from Bates College (Lewiston, ME, US) in 2019 with a BS in Neuroscience.

1. Masters Thesis, Sequentia Biotech

Since I am conducting an industrial thesis, I am currently unable to display my code or discuss my project in substantial detail. However, my thesis generally involves utilizing various machine learning models (random forest, gradient boost, neural nets, etc.) to model both cancer and normal transcriptome RNASeq data. Data science packages in both python (pandas, scikit-learn, keras, tensorflow) as well as R (tidyverse) are used.

2. Exploring MA Public School Data

Using some of the skills that I learned from my masters' program, I conducted a small project using Massachusetts public school district data. One of my closest friends serves as a guidance counselor at a Boston public school, and the main goal was to uncover variables related to college attendance rates, specifically with respect to race and money. This project involved basic data mining / wrangling, exploratory data analysis, and multivariate regression modeling., and I created a simple website to display the project.

3. Differential Gene Expression Analysis

I carried out a differential gene expression analysis as part of a masters course called "Information Extraction in Omics Technology." The Rmarkdown report uses RNASeq count data from Henry et al. (2018) in an attempt to better understand pathways related to noncanonical NF-kB signaling. The report includes quality assessment / preprocessing, differential gene expression analysis, as well as a functional enrichment analysis, and utilizes edgeR, sva, and SummarizedExperiment packages.

4. Bachelors Thesis in Neurobiology

I have linked to a PDF of my year-long laboratory research thesis within the Bates College Neuroscience Department. Investigation of the IRBIT protein was carried out via drug-induced knock-downs and calcium imaging. The goal of the project was to elucidate the IRBIT protein's role in vitro, ultimately expanding our knowledge of dynamic calcium signaling processes in the brain.
